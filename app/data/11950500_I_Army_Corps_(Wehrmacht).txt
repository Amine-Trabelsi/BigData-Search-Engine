I Army Corps (I. Armeekorps) was a corps in the German Army during World War II. It was established by upgrading the former staff of the 1st Division of the Reichsheer at Konigsberg on 1 October 1934.Axis History Factbook At the start of World War 2 the I Army Corps (I. Armeekorps) was assigned to the 3rd Army commanded by Georg von Kuchler. During the invasion of poland it consisted of the 11th infantry division; 61st infantry division and panzer divison kempf. ==Commanders== ==Area of operations== * Poland - September 1939 to May 1940 * France - May 1940 to June 1941 * Eastern Front, Northern Sector - June 1941 to October 1944 * Courland Pocket - October 1944 to May 1945 ==See also== * List of German corps in World War II ==References== ==External links== Army,01 Category:Military units and formations established in 1934 Category:Military units and formations disestablished in 1945
