I Can See Your Voice Thailand is a Thai television mystery music game show series based on the South Korean programme of the same name. Since its premiere on 13 January 2016, it has aired five seasons on Workpoint TV. ==Gameplay== ===Format=== Presented with a group of seven "mystery singers" identified only by their occupation, a guest artist must attempt to eliminate bad singers from the group without ever hearing them sing, assisted by clues and a celebrity panel over the course of four rounds. At the end of the game, the last remaining mystery singer is revealed as either good or bad by means of a duet between them and one of the guest artists. The Thai version has two different formats: *Original format :Under the original format, the guest artist can eliminate one or two mystery singers after each round. The game concludes with the last mystery singer standing that depends on outcome of a duet performance with a guest artist. *Battle format (seasons 3–4) :Under the battle format (adopted from Giọng ải giọng ai), both opponents can eliminate one singer each in the first two rounds, and then both can choose one singer each to join the final performance in the third round. During an interview with CJ ENM format sales director Diane Min in May 2021, she had mentioned Thailand as the first country to air its local adaptation, turning I Can See Your Voice into an international television game show franchise. For the show's fifth anniversary and its subsequent season, it is dubbed as I Can See Your Voice Festival because of its monicker that applies on a randomly-specific theme, affecting the cast and mystery singers. For the sixth season, it is also dubbed as I Can See Your Voice T-pop, with its addition of "T-pop", a shortened term for Thai pop music. ===Filming=== Tapings for the programme took place at the Workpoint Studio in Bang Phun, Mueang, Pathum Thani. In the fourth season, Bigo Live also co-produced the premiere episode. Since from third to fourth season, the programme was filmed under health and safety protocols due to the COVID-19 pandemic. For the sixth season, fan clubs of their respective guest artists as spectators are allowed. ==Broadcast history== I Can See Your Voice Thailand debuted on 13 January 2016. South Korean boy band Got7 became the first foreign guest artists to play in ICSYV franchise, as the second season immediately began airing on 16 August 2017, one week after the first-season finale. In collaboration with Bigo Live, Trinity played in the fourth season premiere on 4 November 2020. As part of the show's fifth anniversary, a subsequent season (dubbed as I Can See Your Voice Festival) premiered on 13 October 2021. After six years of uninterrupted seasonal broadcasts, the show formally concluded with playing on its fifth- season finale on 23 February 2022. After one year and three months of hiatus, the upcoming sixth season (dubbed as I Can See Your Voice T-pop) is scheduled to premiere in 5 July 2023. ===Special episodes and companion events=== For the first two seasons, the series was pre-empted of airing such as mourning period for the death of King of Thailand Bhumibol Adulyadej; then a Memorial Showcase episode was aired following that event. Other highlight episodes that have occasionally held are New Year's Countdown Showcase and Summer, Rainy and Winter Showcases (both for two seasons). After the fourth season premiere, Bigo Live held a companion event titled I Can See My Voice, in which the highest-voting participant would allow to perform a duet alongside one of the guest artists. ==Cast== The series employs a panel of celebrities who assist the guest artist(s) to decipher mystery singers' evidences throughout the game.
